Master's Research

The Role of RENT Complex in Stress-induced Ectopic Activation of the Spindle Assembly Checkpoint in Budding Yeast

Abstract

This study investigates the role of the RENT complex in stress-induced ectopic activation of the Spindle Assembly Checkpoint (SAC) in budding yeast. The research focuses on understanding how TORC1 and its effector SCH9 contribute to SAC activation in cells with defective GPI-APs remodeling.

Key Findings

  • TORC1 and SCH9 are required for SAC activation in cdc1-26 cells
  • Net1p cleavage occurs before cell cycle arrest in dcr2-6 ted1âˆ† cells
  • Proteolytic cleavage of Net1p is specific to the failure to remove EtNP from Man2 of GPI-APs
  • Net1p cleavage is a downstream effect of Clathrin-Mediated Endocytosis (CME) blockage

Research Methods

The study employed various techniques including:

  • Fluorescence microscopy for Mad2p-mNeon coincident foci analysis
  • Western blotting for protein analysis
  • Serial dilution assays for growth analysis
  • Genetic manipulation of yeast strains

Significance

This research contributes to our understanding of:

  • Cell cycle regulation mechanisms
  • Role of GPI-APs in cellular stress response
  • Connection between TORC1 signaling and SAC activation
  • Potential implications for cancer research and treatment
